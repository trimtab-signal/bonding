> "Hello, I am calling to **request a loss mitigation evaluation** for loan number **[XXX]** under **EIN 42-1888158** for **P31 Labs, Inc.** My entity is experiencing a temporary cash-flow interruption due to grant/revenue lag, and I need to discuss forbearance or modification options before the next payment due date. I am requesting a complete loss mitigation package."

## If They Ask Why

> "We are a Georgia nonprofit technology lab in active pilot phase. Revenue is grant‑ and donation‑dependent and has a lag‑cycle. I expect to clear the shortfall within **[X]** months. This is a temporary hardship, not a default risk. Under Regulation X, I am entitled to a complete loss mitigation evaluation."

---

## Key Points to State (in order)

1. **You are requesting forbearance or modification, not making a payment promise.**
2. **You want the agreement in writing** (email or letter) before the due date.
3. **You want to know the exact impact on credit reporting** before you agree — specifically whether it will be reported as "forbearance" or "late."
4. **You want the late-fee waiver stated explicitly.**
5. **Request a Single Point of Contact (SPOC)** for all future communications on this file.

---

## Scripted Responses to Common Push-Back

**"We can only offer forbearance after a missed payment."**  
→ "I understand. I am calling _ahead_ of the due date to avoid a missed payment. If a missed payment is required to open the review, please confirm that in writing and state the date it will be reported."

**"Forbearance will hurt your credit score."**  
→ "Please tell me exactly how this will appear on the business credit report and whether it will be reported as a status of 'forbearance' or 'late.' I need that detail before I can decide."

**"We need financial statements."**  
→ "I can provide a one‑page cash‑flow summary showing the temporary shortfall and the expected recovery date. What specific documents do you need, and is there a fax or secure upload link?"

**"The best I can do is a partial payment plan."**  
→ "I am not able to commit to a partial payment plan at this time. I need a full forbearance or a formal modification. If that is not available, please escalate to your loss‑mitigation supervisor and give me the direct line."

## Escalation Path

| Step | Action                       | Target                                                                   |
| ---- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 1    | Ask for supervisor           | "Can you connect me to your loss mitigation supervisor?"                 |
| 2    | Request written confirmation | "Please email me the forbearance terms within 24 hours."                 |
| 3    | Formal written request       | Send certified mail + email to the servicer's loss mitigation department |
| 4    | If denied, contact           | HUD-approved housing counselor (free) or legal aid                       |

---

## Post-Call Actions

- [ ] Log: agent name, reference #, date, time, and SPOC name
- [ ] Confirm the SPOC's direct contact info (email + phone)
- [ ] Request email confirmation of what was discussed within 24 hours
- [ ] If oral agreement reached, follow up with formal email restating terms and send to SPOC
- [ ] Set calendar reminder 3 days before next due date to confirm status
- [ ] Send all required documents via the method the servicer specified (secure upload, fax, or email)

---

## Fallback: If Forbearance Is Denied

1. Request a **formal loan modification** (per HAMP or in-house program).
2. Explore **bridge funding** from personal reserves or trusted partners.
3. Contact a **Georgia legal aid society** for free foreclosure‑prevention advice.
4. As a last resort, **short sale** or **deed in lieu** — but these should be advisor‑guided, not done alone.

---

## Notes

- Keep tone calm, factual, unemotional.
- Do not admit inability to pay; state _cash‑flow lag_ instead.
- Do not agree to anything verbally that you cannot afford.
- This script is for informational purposes; consult legal counsel before signing.
