## 1. Executive Summary (200 words)

P31 Labs, Inc. requests **$50,000** to launch the **Neuro-Tech Career Catalyst**, a 12-month program that trains neurodivergent young adults (ages 18-30) in open-source software development, edge computing (Raspberry Pi), and human-computer interaction. Participants build real-world assistive-tech projects while earning industry-recognized certifications. The program culminates in a paid internship placement with Georgia tech employers.

This initiative addresses two gaps: (a) ~85% unemployment/underemployment among neurodivergent adults (CDC), and (b) growing demand for skilled tech workers in Georgia's fintech and logistics sectors.

## 3. Problem Statement

- Neurodivergent individuals (autism, ADHD, dyslexia) possess exceptional pattern-recognition, logic, and focus — ideal for software engineering.
- Traditional hiring processes filter them out (resume gaps, non-standard interviews, lack of accommodations).
- Existing bootcamps (TechBridge, CodePath) lack neuro-affirming curricula or mentorship.

**Target:** 12 young adults in Camden County and surrounding areas who are unemployed or underemployed with demonstrated interest in technology.

---

## 4. Proposed Solution

**Neuro-Tech Career Catalyst** — three-phase program:

1. **Weeks 1-8 (Bootcamp):** Python, Git, Docker, Raspberry Pi OS, basic electronics. Neurodivergent instructors, flexible pacing, sensory-friendly spaces.
2. **Weeks 9-24 (Project Lab):** Participants build assistive-tech devices (AAC interfaces, environmental controls, sensory-regulation tools) using the K4-based framework and open-source stack.
3. **Weeks 25-48 (Internship):** Paid 20-hr/week remote or hybrid internships with partner companies (Georgia firms in logistics, fintech, healthcare tech).

**Key differentiators:**
- Neurodivergent-led design
- No student debt — free to participants
- Open-source assets — all curricula, code, and hardware designs released under open license
